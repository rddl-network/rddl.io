Decentralization Layers in Energy Systems

Monitoring and Control Across Three Scales

1

Within Houses

Decentralized energy assets at the household level, enabling individual buildings to generate, store, and manage their own energy.

Monitoring & Control Focus
Individual asset optimization and household energy management

Key Components

  • Photovoltaic (PV) Systems
  • Battery Storage
  • Heat Pumps
  • Cooling Systems
  • Smart Home Devices
  • Energy Management Systems
2

Within Energy Communities

Coordinated management and optimization across multiple households, balancing producers, consumers, and storage within a local community.

Monitoring & Control Focus
Community-level coordination between producers, consumers, and storage systems

Managed Elements

  • Energy Producers (Multiple PV systems)
  • Energy Consumers (Households, small businesses)
  • Shared Storage Systems
  • Local Trading Mechanisms
  • Community Energy Optimization
  • Peer-to-Peer Energy Exchange
3

Distribution System Operator (DSO) Level

Large-scale coordination across the distribution network, managing bigger producers, consumers, and storage systems for grid stability and efficiency.

Monitoring & Control Focus
Grid-level optimization and coordination of large-scale assets and communities

Managed Elements

  • Large-scale Producers (Solar farms, wind parks)
  • Industrial Consumers
  • Grid-scale Storage Systems
  • Multiple Energy Communities
  • Distribution Network Management
  • Grid Balancing & Stability
